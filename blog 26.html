Cervical Cancer Symptoms and Treatment
Cervical cancer is when the cells of the cervix start to change. This causes a lump or bump to form on the cervix and on the back of the neck. These can be felt or noticed with the hand. Causes If the tissues are cancerous they can spread to the lymph nodes, uterus, fallopian tube and ovaries and liver. Doctors classify cervical cancer into 2 categories. 1.Cervical pre-cancer Cervical pre-cancer occurs if the cells in the cervix or cervical area are growing or changing before the cells in the uterus or fallopian tube. It can usually be treated by surgically removing these cells. 2.Cervical cancer Cervical cancer occurs when the cancer has spread from the cervix or cervical area into the uterus or fallopian tube and other organs in the pelvis.













Regular medical checkups for screening and prevention of cervical cancer is the best way to prevent cervical cancer. Screening is done by doctor who injects an HPV-specific probe that detects cells found in cervical cells. The same probe is inserted in the cervix of the person being screened. Today many preventive measures are being used to prevent cervical cancer. Regular Pap smears, HPV vaccine, birth control pills and cervical cancer vaccination are few of them.
