Fungal Nail Infection: Causes, Symptoms, Treatments

Fungi can be present in or on any oart of the body with various
germs and bacterias. It can also over grows and result us an
Infection.

A fungal infection either in a nail or a toenail or both is called
Onychomycosis also called tinea unguium. Fungal infection grows overtime
so any immediate difference in the way your nail looks or feels may be
too subtle to notice at first.

Types of fungal infection
1)  Distal subungual infection
This is the most common type of fungal infection which
develops in both nail and toenail.When infected, the outer
edge of the nail has a jagged appearance with white and/or
yellow streaks across the nail.

2)  White superficial infection
This mainly effect the toenail. Effects the top layer of
These patches cover entire nail. Which becomes rough, soft
and prone to crumbling.

3)  Proximal subungual infection
These are uncommon but can infect both fingernail and toenail
Yellow spots appear at the base of the nail as the infection
spreads upward.

4)  Candida infection
It can damage nail by a prior injury or infection. Commonly it infectes
fingernail . It often occurs in people who frequently soak their hands
in water.

Treatment
 1)  Ciclopirox (Penlac)
Ciclopirox (Penlac) is a nail polish that
kills the fungus on the nail.

2)  Medicated Nail Cream
Medicated nail cream are best for thin nail
Docotrs also prescribe it oftenly.

3)  Surgery
Doctors may advice temporary removal of nail in some serious
Conditions.

2)  Tea Tree Oil
