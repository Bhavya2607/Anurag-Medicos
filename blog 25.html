How To Prevent Tooth Decay, Sensitivity, And Cavities:A Guide For Healthy Teeth

The Damage to the teeth's surface or enamel is called tooth decay .
It happens when bacteria in your mouth acid attacks the enamel. Tooth
decay can lead to cavities which are holes in your mouth.


Prevention of tooth decay How do you prevent tooth decay? Clean and polish your teeth twice a day with a
gentle toothbrush Start to eat flaxseed, coconut, or sunflower seeds. Do not use
food that contains a lot of sugar or sweeteners like gum or candy. Instead, try
fruits and vegetables. Use a toothpaste containing fluoride. It makes your teeth
stronger and prevents tooth decay. Include a lot of fresh fruits and vegetables in
your diet. Reduce stress. Take adequate amount of rest. Eat a high fibre diet. Sugary
drinks like sodas, juices and milk is generally recommended not to be consumed because
they contain sugar. Avoid and eat foods which have added preservatives. Use water for
drinking, brushing your teeth and cooking.




Prevention of sensitivity
Being sensitive to your toothpaste can make your teeth more sensitive and leads to
sensitivity. Experts advise using toothpaste on a toothbrush, which will reduce the
exposure of toothpaste to your tooth enamel. How To Prevent Tooth sensitivity To avoid
tooth sensitivity, experts say, "Do not scrub your teeth with the toothpaste because this
will cause cavities by introducing bacteria from your mouth into your teeth." Instead,
brush your teeth by using the toothbrush. Routine dental check-up Sensitivity and sensitivity
of your teeth are two different things. One can be caused by something external or by something
in your mouth. These teeth sensitivity problems will disappear once you get a routine dental
check-up done.













Prevention of cavities
Good oral hygiene is the best way to prevent tooth decay and sensitivity. It's easy to maintain
your dental hygiene with the following tips. Clean your teeth twice daily with a toothbrush and
clean mouth wash, with fluoride toothpaste. Brush twice daily for 2 minutes. This is the best
method to eliminate the plaque on your teeth which can lead to decay. Brush every day for a minimum
of 2 minutes using a soft brush. Ensure you brush both the upper and lower teeth at the same time.
Floss your teeth after every meal. Brush between each meal using a different toothbrush Using a proper
toothpaste with fluoride. You can buy the toothpaste in the pharmacy or get it from the dentist.



History
Cavities, also referred to as dental caries, have caused tooth pain for millions of years.
Fossils from the Australopithecus species reveal some of the earliest dental caries from 1.1
million to 4.4 million years ago. Paleolithic and Mesolithic skulls also show signs of cavities.
